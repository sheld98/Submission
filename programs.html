Programs

Membership Cover

Our welfare programs provide financial assistance during bereavement and health emergencies.

Bereavement Contributions

    Member's Death:

  • Ksh. 2,000 deducted per head, and each member contributes Ksh. 1,000.

    • Spouse/Parent's Death:

  • Ksh. 1,000 deducted per head, and each member contributes Ksh. 500.

    • Child's Death:

  • Ksh. 500 deducted per head, and each member contributes Ksh. 500.

Health Support Mechanism

  • Critical Health Situations: The leadership convenes a meeting to assess the severity and decide the way forward.
  • Non-Critical Health Matters: Each member contributes Ksh. 500.

Goodwill Activities

We support members during celebratory events such as weddings, graduations, and other milestones.

  • Contributions: Members contribute up to Ksh. 500 per event, unless otherwise decided by the General Assembly.
  • Notification: Members must notify the leadership in writing to request support for goodwill activities.

Financial Facility

We offer two types of loans to support members financially:

Eligibility Criteria

  • Members must have been part of the association for at least six months before applying for a loan.
  • Loans shall not exceed 75% of the applicant's total savings in the association.

Loan Types

  • Flash Loan:
    • Repayable within one month.
    • Suitable for minor emergencies or temporary cash flow challenges.
    • Requires no guarantors but may involve a quick credit assessment.
  • Short-Term Boost Loan:
    • Repayable over three months.
    • Ideal for addressing more significant short-term obligations.
    • Requires two guarantors whose combined security shares correspond to the loan amount.

Interest Rates

  • Loans carry the lowest possible interest rate, determined annually by the General Assembly.
  • Interest collected is shared among members at the AGM as part of dividends.

Application Process

  • Members must submit a written application detailing the purpose of the loan and their repayment capacity.
  • Applications are reviewed by the Treasurer and approved by the Executive Committee within seven days.

Default Management

  • In case of default, the leadership notifies the member and proposes a repayment extension or installment plan.
  • Guarantors for Short-Term Boost Loans may be held liable for unpaid amounts.
